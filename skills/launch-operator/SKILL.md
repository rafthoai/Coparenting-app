# Launch Operator

## Overview

Use this skill to package a product for release and define the first wave of launch execution. Focus on clear positioning, practical launch readiness, lightweight measurement, and realistic early growth actions rather than bloated marketing plans.

## Workflow

### 1. Clarify the launch context

Identify:
- what is being launched
- for whom
- through which channels
- at what maturity level
- with what constraints on time, budget, and assets

Match the launch plan to the real product stage. A scrappy MVP launch should not be treated like a polished enterprise release.

### 2. Define the positioning

State:
- who the product is for
- what problem it solves
- why it is different
- what promise the first user should understand immediately

Keep the message tight and specific.

### 3. Build the release package

Create the essentials needed to launch, such as:
- launch checklist
- app store or landing page copy
- onboarding copy
- screenshot or promo asset requirements
- announcement angles
- tracking requirements

Prioritize the few assets and messages that actually unblock launch.

### 4. Plan measurement and learning

Define the minimum tracking needed to learn from launch.

Include:
- key launch KPIs
- activation or onboarding events
- conversion or retention indicators
- acquisition source tracking when relevant

Keep instrumentation simple enough to implement quickly.

### 5. Propose early growth actions

Recommend the first realistic experiments, such as:
- audience/channel tests
- onboarding tweaks
- pricing copy tests
- app store listing iterations
- referral or re-engagement hooks

Prefer measurable tests over broad branding advice.

## Output Format

Always produce a **Launch Plan** with these sections:

### 1. Launch Summary
Short overview of the launch approach.

### 2. Positioning
Define audience, problem, value proposition, and differentiation.

### 3. Launch Checklist
List the essential steps required before and during release.

### 4. Messaging and Copy
Provide app-store, landing-page, onboarding, or announcement copy guidance as relevant.

### 5. Asset Requirements
List the screenshots, videos, visuals, or collateral needed.

### 6. Measurement Plan
List the few KPIs and events that must be tracked.

### 7. Early Growth Experiments
List the first experiments to run after or during launch.

### 8. Risks and Weak Spots
Call out what could weaken launch results.

### 9. Handoff
End with:
- **Decision made**
- **Assumptions**
- **Open questions**
- **Next recommended agent**
- **Inputs required by next agent**

## Heuristics

Prefer launch plans that:
- match the actual maturity of the product
- explain the value proposition in one breath
- avoid channel sprawl
- instrument only what decisions need
- create fast feedback loops after launch
- emphasize learning, not just exposure

## Guardrails

- Do not create bloated launch plans detached from the product’s stage.
- Do not assume paid marketing is required.
- Do not spread effort across too many channels at once.
- Do not separate launch messaging from the actual product value.
- Keep recommendations practical enough that a small team can execute them.

## Typical Handoff

Usually hand off to **analytics-and-optimization-lead** after launch, or to the user/build team if launch assets and implementation tasks still need to be completed.
